What it is

The problem uses comparison language with fractions: "1/2 of A is $15 more than 1/3 of B." Students must carefully translate each phrase into a mathematical expression, then form an equation. The word "than" is the pivot — what comes before it is the larger quantity.

Signal phrases — train students to spot

  • "1/2 of Ali's money is $15 more than 1/3 of Baba's"
  • "2/5 of the boys is 8 fewer than 1/2 of the girls"
  • "... more than ..." or "... less than ..." with fractions on both sides
  • The word "than" splits the larger and smaller quantities

🔥 Common trap

Students write "$45 + $15 = 1/3 of Baba" — they added instead of subtracted! The phrase "X is $15 more than Y" means X = Y + 15, so Y = X - 15. Train them to circle "than" and ask: "Which side is bigger?"

🌱 Teaching tip

Use the "is more than" frame: [A] is [gap] more than [B] → A = B + gap. Have students substitut the fractional expressions first, then solve. For weaker students, draw the two bars side by side with the $15 gap visually marked.
