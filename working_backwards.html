What it is

The problem describes a sequence of operations (add, subtract, multiply, divide) that leads to a final number. Students work from the end back to the start, reversing each operation: addition becomes subtraction, subtraction becomes addition, multiplication becomes division, division becomes multiplication. This is essential for P5 Branching (Working Backwards).

Signal phrases — train students to spot

  • "Ali doubled his money, then added $10. He had $50"
  • "A number is multiplied by 3, then 12 is subtracted. The result is 18"
  • "After giving away half, then 5 more, 7 remained"
  • A sequence of changes with the final result known

🔥 Common trap

Students try to work forwards: "If he had X, then doubled..." They get stuck because they don't know the start. Others reverse operations incorrectly: "Spent $15" means add $15 back (correct), but some subtract again. Train them to write the operation chain and check each reverse: ×2 becomes ÷2, +15 becomes -15.

🌱 Teaching tip

Draw a "forward chain" with arrows, then draw the reverse chain below it. Label each reverse operation explicitly. For younger students, use a table: | Step | Forward | Reverse |. This builds the "undo" intuition that carries into algebra.
