What it is

Both quantities change by different amounts (e.g., Ali spends $20, Baba gains $10). Neither the total, difference, nor individual amounts stay the same. The only constant is the ratio relationship after the change. Students set up a "before/after" table in units, express the new ratio as an equation, and solve for u.

Signal phrases — train students to spot

  • "Ali spent $20 and Baba received $10"
  • "After A gave $15 to B, the ratio became..."
  • "The ratio was 3:2 at first. After Ali lost some and Baba found some..."
  • Both items change — no unchanged quantity to anchor on

🔥 Common trap

Students try to find an "unchanged" quantity that doesn't exist. Both items changed! The only anchor is the new ratio. Train them to write the "after" expressions first, then form the ratio equation.

🌱 Teaching tip

Always use the same unit letter (e.g., 'u') for the before state. Write "3u - 20" not "3 units - 20" — keeping the algebraic form makes cross-multiplication natural. Some students find the bar model (Method 2) more intuitive; let them choose.

💡 Show full mark scheme (4 marks)

M1: Express after amounts as (3u - 20) and (2u + 10)

M1: Set up equation: (3u - 20)/(2u + 10) = 2/3 (or equivalent)

M1: Solve: 9u - 60 = 4u + 20 → 5u = 80

A1: u = 16, Ali at first = $48
