What it is

P6 problems where ratios change after a transfer — money moves from A to B, items are added or removed, and the new ratio is given. Students draw before-and-after bars, keep the unit system consistent, and use the transfer amount to bridge the two states.

Signal phrases — train students to spot

  • "The ratio was 5:3. After Ali gave Baba $40, the ratio became 3:2"
  • "Some boys left the hall, and the ratio of boys to girls changed to..."
  • "A transfer happens, then a new ratio is stated"
  • The total is unchanged in internal-transfer problems

Common student traps

⚠️ Trap 1: Mixing up the two unit systems

Students use "u" for before AND after, forgetting the ratio change means the units are different. Before units (u) and after units (p) are not the same size. They must be aligned via the total or the transfer amount.

⚠️ Trap 2: Treating the $40 as a ratio part

Students write "5u − 3u = $40" or "$40 = 2u." The $40 is an absolute dollar amount, not a ratio part. It must be connected to the unit system through the total or by cross-multiplication.

⚠️ Trap 3: Forgetting that total is unchanged

In internal-transfer problems, the total stays the same. Students sometimes add the $40 to the total instead of recognising it's just moved from one person to another.

How to teach it

💡 Three teaching anchors

1. Always draw BEFORE and AFTER side by side. Never try to morph one bar into another — students get lost. Two separate diagrams, two separate unit systems, then bridge them.

2. Label the transfer with a bright colour. The $40 (or whatever moves) should be drawn as a physical block with an arrow. Students need to see it as a thing that moves.

3. Force the "total check." Before total must equal after total in internal transfer. If they don't match, the student has mixed up their units.

Practice: The ratio of boys to girls in a hall was 7:5. After 24 boys left, the ratio became 3:5. How many boys were there at first?
▸ Click to reveal answer

Before: boys = 7u, girls = 5u. Total = 12u.

After: boys = 7u − 24, girls = 5u. Ratio = 3:5.

So (7u − 24) : 5u = 3 : 5 → 5(7u − 24) = 3(5u) → 35u − 120 = 15u → 20u = 120 → u = 6

Boys at first = 7 × 6 = 42 boys
