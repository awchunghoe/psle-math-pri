What it is

"Fraction of a set" means finding part of a group. 1/4 of 20 apples = ? The key is to unitise: find 1 unit first (divide by the denominator), then multiply by the numerator. This is different from Part-Whole (P5) where the fraction represents a ratio — here the fraction acts on a known total to find a part.

Signal phrases — train students to spot

  • "There are 24 pupils. 1/4 of them are boys"
  • "2/5 of the 30 stickers were red"
  • "She ate 3/8 of the 40 cookies"
  • A fraction acts on a known total number of items

⚠️ Common trap

Students see "1/4 of 24" and do 24 ÷ 1 × 4 = 96. They flipped the operation. Train them: "of" means divide by the denominator FIRST, then multiply by the numerator. The denominator splits the total.

💡 Teaching tip

Draw the items as a grid. Physically circle groups. "If I split 24 into 4 equal piles, how many in each pile?" This grounds the abstract fraction in concrete counting.

⬆️ P5 Extension: This concept evolves into Part-Whole — where you know a PART and its fraction, and must find the TOTAL (reverse thinking).
