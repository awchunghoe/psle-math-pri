What it is

The problem gives you two facts: the total of both quantities, and the difference between them. Students draw bars to visualise, then apply the classic formulas: Bigger = (Total + Gap) / 2 and Smaller = (Total - Gap) / 2.

Signal phrases — train students to spot

  • "Ali and Baba had 120 marbles altogether"
  • "Ali had 30 more than Baba"
  • "The total was $80 and the difference was $20"
  • Two numbers: sum known, difference known

Question: Ali and Baba had 120 marbles altogether. Ali had 30 more marbles than Baba. How many marbles did each of them have?

🔥 Common trap

Students do 120 ÷ 2 = 60 each, then add/subtract 30 to get 90 and 30. This gives the right total (120) but the wrong difference (60)! The formulas must be applied together: both numbers use (Total +/- Gap) / 2.

🌱 Teaching tip

Have students memorise the two formulas as a pair: "Bigger gets the plus, smaller gets the minus." Draw the "remove the gap" visual — when you chop off the extra 30 from Ali, both bars become equal (Baba's amount). This makes the division by 2 intuitive.
